Paban Bhuyan

Master Student in Climate Science & Technology.

Research Interest includes Ocean dynamics, Submesoscale Processes, Ocean Current, Biogeochemistry, Upwelling systems, and Physical-Biological ocean modeling.

I am Paban, a fourth-year PhD student in the Air-Sea Lab at the University of Connecticut, guided by Dr. Leonel Romero at UConn and Dr. Cesar Rocha at USP. My scholarly pursuits delve into the oceanic currents that astronomer and oceanographer Paul Scully-Power observed from the vastness of space, known as submesoscale ocean processes (as cited in Oceans to Orbit). For my research, I employ robotic vehicles that venture into the deep, exploring the oceanic frontiers we command them towards. Presently, my focus lies on these submesoscale phenomena near oceanic fronts—those regions where the properties of water undergo sharp transitions. In addition to my primary research, I engage in the craft of DIY science, constructing instruments such as wave sensors. Pictured here is the wave buoy sensor we engineered, originally an undergraduate project by Vicki You, alongside Akaawase Bernard and Leonel Romero. We also devised a pressure sensor, the fruition of Hiryu Shinand’s project, again in collaboration with Akaawase Bernard and Leonel Romero. My academic journey began with a master's degree in Climate Science and Technology from IIT Bhubaneswar. Following that, I served as a Junior Research Fellow for a year at IIT Delhi within the Department of Applied Mechanics—a fascinating domain, though my undergraduate roots were in Civil Engineering. Yet, my ardor for geophysical fluid dynamics led me to traverse into this realm.
