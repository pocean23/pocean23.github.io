Paban Bhuyan

Master Student in Climate Science & Technology.

Research Interest includes Ocean dynamics, Ocean Current, Biogeochemistry, Upwelling systems, and Physical-Biological ocean modeling.

Boundary currents are essential for maintaining the balance of heat and salt over a domain and for the transport of nutrients, which is needed for the marine organisms to thrive. I use HF Radar current, Drifter, Satellite Altimetry derived (Geostrophic current) SSHA, SST, SSS to study boundary current along the western Bay of Bengal. My research concentrates on characterizing the East India Coastal Current and associated processes along with it. My research also includes the study of Bio-geochemisttry of the coastal ocean. Bay of Bengal being the data sporadic region for ocean colour products due to cloud cover over this domain most of the year. I intend to inderctely infer Biological activity and upweliing system from satellite track SSHA, HF Radar, Wind, and from other physical mechnanisms like Ekman transprort and Ekman Pumping.In addition to that I use model to study these mechanisms affecting the biological activities.

I am a Master student at School of Earth, Ocean and Climate Sciences, the Indian Institute of Technology Bhubaneswar.
